# F15.5b — Breakout rooms

> **Everything still open across P15 is consolidated in [`f15-followups.md`](./f15-followups.md).**

> The optional refinement on F15.5a. A breakout is a period of TIME; usually the whole group
> answers the same questionnaire individually and there are no rooms at all. Sometimes the group
> splits: each room takes its own dedicated questionnaire, and works either individually or through
> one person writing for everybody.

## Confirmed decisions

- **Rooms are a separate table, not columns on the step.** The roomless common case stays entirely
  untouched, and nothing has to interpret "roomId is null" as a mode.

- **A room's questionnaire is optional.** Null inherits the breakout step's, which is still the
  common case even when rooms exist — splitting a group does not always mean asking different
  questions.

- **`scribe` mode means ONE session for the whole room.** The others are present and watching with
  no session of their own. A room that talks an answer through together has one answer, not six —
  and six near-identical copies would also make the k-anonymity support counts meaningless.

- **The pen is first-come, not a separate role-assignment step.** A room of people looking at each
  other deciding who types is exactly the friction a timed breakout cannot afford. The picker says
  which they are getting — "Take the pen" versus "Join and watch" — so it is never a surprise.

- **`currentRoomId` sits on the RUN as well as `roomId` on the leg.** A participant watching a
  scribe write has no leg of their own, and the facilitator still needs to see them placed.

- **Rooms are synthesised SEPARATELY.** They may have answered different questionnaires, so
  combining them would resolve fills against the wrong data-slot vocabulary and silently drop most
  of them — the same failure per-step report scoping exists to prevent. A roomless breakout is the
  single-null-room case and takes the identical code path.

- **You cannot choose a room during GRACE.** Arriving at a room with seconds left, to a
  questionnaire you have not started, is worse than being told you missed it. Grace is for
  finishing, not starting.

- **Every new pointer is unmodelled (UG-1).** Deleting a room from a draft agenda must not cascade
  away the answers given in it. Only room→step is a real relation, because that one is pure config.

## What shipped

**Schema** (migration `20260720140000`, hand-stripped, indexes verified):
`AppExperienceBreakoutRoom` (name, ordinal, optional questionnaire/version, mode),
`AppExperienceRunLeg.roomId`, `AppExperienceRun.currentRoomId`, `AppExperienceInsight.roomId`.

**Lib**: `BREAKOUT_ROOM_MODES`, `BreakoutRoomView`, `canChooseRoom`.

**Service**: `loadBreakoutRooms` (with live occupancy and whether the pen is taken), `chooseRoom`
(which also claims the pen), room-aware `ensureBreakoutSession`, per-room `synthesiseAndStore`.

**Routes**: `GET|POST /meetings/:id/rooms`.

**UI**: `BreakoutRoomPicker`, shown only when the running breakout has rooms; the breakout meta
fields (duration in minutes, briefing, synthesis focus) on the step form, revealed only for a
`breakout` step.

## Deferred

- Authoring rooms themselves has no UI yet — the model, service and participant flow are complete,
  but rooms must currently be created through the API. The step form gained the breakout meta;
  a rooms editor beside it is the natural next piece.
- Handing the pen to somebody else mid-breakout.
