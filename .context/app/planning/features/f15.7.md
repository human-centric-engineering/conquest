# F15.7 — Explaining Experiences

> **Everything still open across P15 is consolidated in [`f15-followups.md`](./f15-followups.md).**

> P15 shipped a large runtime with almost no explanation of itself. Field-level help was dense —
> 72 `<FieldHelp>` popovers — but there was exactly **one** conceptual explainer in the whole admin
> area, and the journey was rendered as a flat list everywhere even though a switcher branches.

## The problem, stated precisely

Two gaps, and they are different:

1. **No conceptual layer.** `EXPERIENCE_KIND_DESCRIPTIONS` is two sentences and the list page has
   one ⓘ. The Steps, Routing, Runs, Reports and Meetings tabs explained nothing beyond a subtitle.
   An admin could fill every field correctly without ever understanding what they were building.
2. **The list misrepresents the shape.** Overview and Steps both render an `<ol>`. For a
   facilitated meeting that is honest — it really is a sequence. For an `agentic_switcher` it hides
   the entire structure: that rule order is significant, that the selector only sees what the rules
   did not settle, and that a fallback always catches the rest.

## Confirmed decisions

- **Reuse the Behind-the-Scenes canvas; do not build a second one.** `ReadOnlyCanvas` and the
  `diagram()`/`node()` authoring helpers were already proven across 15 diagrams. A parallel React
  Flow surface would have drifted from it within a release.

- **Nothing goes in `/admin/orchestration/workflows`.** That list holds executable `AiWorkflow`
  rows. Experiences are hand-written TypeScript orchestration — a non-executable entry inside an
  editable surface invites someone to press Run. The Behind-the-Scenes picker exists precisely to
  hold that distinction, and it got a sixth category instead.

- **Generic diagrams AND a live one.** They answer different questions — "how does this kind work?"
  versus "what have I actually built?" — and the live one is what stays true as the author edits.

- **`_meta.settings` gained a `scope`.** Experience settings live in `ExperienceSettingsShape` on
  `AppExperience`, not `QuestionnaireConfigShape`. The cheap alternative was to omit the field on
  experience nodes; that would have cost the operator the "where do I change this?" answer, which
  is the only reason `_meta.settings` exists. Defaulting to `'questionnaire'` left all 15 existing
  diagrams untouched, and the integrity test now pins **both** blobs.

- **Switcher examples span three unrelated domains** — triage, escalating depth, role-branching.
  The switcher is general-purpose conditional routing; a single commercial example would narrow
  what authors imagine it can do and leak a sales framing many customers do not share.

## What shipped

- Three curated diagrams: `experience-switcher`, `experience-meeting`, `experience-run-lifecycle`,
  filed under a new `experiences` category (last — an experience spans every earlier stage).
- `buildExperienceDiagram()` — pure, client-safe, never throws, renders dangling pointers visibly.
- `/admin/experiences/[id]/how-it-works` — live diagram + blockers, the generic diagram for the
  kind, and worked examples.
- `examples.ts` — five worked examples, also shown compactly under the create form's kind selector.
- `?workflow=<slug>` deep links into Behind the Scenes (unknown slug degrades to the default).
- 12 new unit tests on the builder; integrity test extended for the new agents and setting scope.

## Findings worth remembering

- **`report` is a vestigial step kind.** It is in `EXPERIENCE_STEP_KINDS`, has a label, and is
  offered in the step form for both kinds — but a grep for any runtime consumer returns **nothing**.
  The run report is enqueued from `concludeRun`; `routableSteps` only selects `branch`. Drawing the
  journey is what made this visible. The diagram says "authored marker only" rather than inventing
  a role. **Open decision: wire it up or remove it from the form** — selectable-but-inert is the
  worst of the three states. Tracked in `f15-followups.md`.

- The three Experience agents are real and seeded but build prompts in code, so they are
  allowlisted as uncatalogued and correctly show no Prompt tab. Their slugs live in
  `experiences/constants.ts`, which the integrity test previously did not scan at all.
