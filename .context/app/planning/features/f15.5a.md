# F15.5a — Facilitated meetings

> **Everything still open across P15 is consolidated in [`f15-followups.md`](./f15-followups.md).**

> A facilitator creates a meeting, puts the join link on screen, runs an agenda of timed
> breakouts, and walks the room through what the synthesis surfaced. Participants join at
> `/m/<joinRef>`, answer during a breakout, and see nothing but a waiting state between them.

## What a breakout actually is

**A period of TIME in a meeting, not a place.** Participants go and have their own chats against a
questionnaire; then the answers are aggregated and synthesised for the facilitator to walk the room
through. Rooms — where each room works on its own dedicated questionnaire, individually or through
a single scribe — are a refinement _within_ a breakout, deferred to F15.5b.

This corrected an earlier reading. The kind description ("the **same** short questionnaire run by
many people at once") and the readiness blocker ("at least one breakout **step with a questionnaire
attached**") implied two different data models; the resolution is that a breakout step is an
**agenda item**, and `ordinal` is the agenda order.

## Confirmed decisions

- **`AppExperienceRun` cannot carry a meeting.** A run is ONE respondent's journey; a meeting is
  the shared, live fact that a group is doing this together right now. Hence `AppExperienceMeeting`
  above the runs, owning what belongs to the occurrence: which breakout is live, and its clock.
  Runs attach via a nullable `meetingId` (null for a switcher run, which travels alone).

- **Duration lives on the STEP; the clock lives on the MEETING.** The same agenda is run many times
  — a quarterly offsite, a different room each time. The step says "this breakout is meant to take
  12 minutes"; the meeting says "it started at 14:03".

- **`breakoutEndsAt` is stored, not derived.** An author editing the duration mid-meeting must not
  retroactively move a clock the room is already watching.

- **The facilitator drives; the clock advises.** A timer never ends a breakout. A room running
  three minutes over is normal facilitation, and closing a questionnaire mid-sentence because a
  clock expired would be actively hostile. `isOverrunning` is a DISPLAY state; only an explicit
  `endBreakout` closes one.

- **The facilitator SETS the length, the step only suggests it.** `AppExperienceStep.durationSeconds`
  is the authored default the console pre-fills; `AppExperienceMeeting.breakoutDurationSeconds`
  records what they actually chose for this occurrence. The room in front of them decides whether
  twelve minutes is right today.

- **A breakout has THREE phases, not two.** `running` → `grace` → `closed`. The grace window
  (`breakoutGraceSeconds`, default 30) exists because the clock ending and the room being done are
  not the same moment. During grace a participant may FINISH and submit what they are mid-way
  through but not begin something new — an answer started after the bell will not be part of the
  conversation the room is about to have, and cutting both off at once loses answers people had
  already written. `participantWindow` returns `canAnswer` and `canSubmit` separately for exactly
  this reason.

- **Clock boundaries favour the participant.** Exactly AT `breakoutEndsAt` is still `running`;
  exactly at the grace boundary is still `grace`. Nobody loses a submission to a rounding edge. An
  unparseable clock fails OPEN (treated as untimed) rather than silently shutting a room out.

- **Grace and duration are frozen at breakout start**, like `breakoutEndsAt` — a room mid-sentence
  must not lose its remaining seconds because somebody edited a setting.

- **Where the analysis appears is configurable per experience.** `respondentInsightDisplay`
  (`none` | `tab` | `modal`) governs whether respondents see the synthesis on their OWN screen and
  how; `consoleDisplayMode` (`standard` | `presentation`) governs whether the console renders for a
  laptop or for a projector / screenshare. Both are settings rather than device guesses, because
  nothing about a viewport says whether this console is the facilitator's private working surface
  or the only thing forty people on a Zoom call can see. `respondentInsightDisplay` defaults to
  `none`: a facilitated meeting is a room looking at one thing together, and putting the analysis
  on forty phones by default changes that without being asked.

- **No second concurrent breakout.** Two open at once would split the room's answers across
  questionnaires with no way to tell afterwards which period an answer belonged to — and synthesis
  is scoped per breakout.

- **Participants cannot answer between breakouts.** The room is listening to the facilitator, not
  typing; an answer arriving then would land in whichever breakout started next.

- **`briefing` and `synthesisFocus` are separate from `purpose`.** `purpose` is written for the
  ROUTING SELECTOR to read; `briefing` is what the facilitator says to the room. Different
  audiences — one field serving both would be written for neither.

- **k-anonymity is structural.** A meeting synthesis is read ALOUD to the room it came from — the
  audience and the subjects are the same people, sitting together, who know who said what five
  minutes ago. "A tension between two of you" is not anonymous. `meetsSupportThreshold` refuses any
  threshold below 2 whatever the caller passes, `supportCount` is stored so the gate re-applies on
  READ (raising the setting later makes an existing meeting safer without regeneration), and
  `visibleToRespondents` can never override the gate.

- **Suppression reports a COUNT to the facilitator, never the withheld statements.** An operator
  should know their synthesis was thinned — otherwise they read the gaps as "everyone agreed" — but
  being able to read the suppressed findings would be reading exactly the attributable ones the
  gate exists to prevent.

- **The synthesis reads DATA SLOTS, never raw chat.** Fills, rationales, refinement history and the
  questionnaire's own background — nothing else. That is the point of the data-slot layer: it is
  the semantic answer vocabulary, already normalised away from how any individual happened to
  phrase things. Feeding transcripts to something read aloud to the room would drag in verbatim
  wording, side remarks and disclosures never meant for the group, and would make
  de-identification a post-hoc filtering problem instead of a structural property of the input.

- **Movement is first-class material.** `refinementHistory` carries `previousValue → newValue`, the
  rationale for the change, and the confidence either side. A position that MOVED during a
  conversation is often the most interesting thing in the room — "four of you started somewhere
  else, and here is what shifted you" is a better facilitation prompt than any static tally. A bare
  value swap with no rationale is dropped: it tells no story and only pads the material.

- **Participants are indices, never people.** `P1`, `P2`, … local to one breakout's material. The
  synthesiser must be able to tell that two positions came from the SAME person — otherwise it
  cannot distinguish a genuine split from one person contradicting themselves — but it never sees
  a name, an email or a session id. A test asserts no session id appears anywhere in the material.

- **The denominator is who COMPLETED the breakout, not who answered a given slot.** Deriving it
  from fills would quietly inflate every proportion into "everyone agreed" when half the room said
  nothing.

- **An inferred fill is marked inferred.** A synthesis that treated the pipeline's guesses as the
  room's own words would report inferences back to the people who never said them.

- **Below the support floor, no model call is made at all.** The gate would suppress every finding
  anyway, so running it spends money to produce nothing; the caller says "not enough responses
  yet", which is also the honest thing to tell a facilitator watching a room of three.

- **The synthesis output is SPOKEN, not filed** — which is what shapes the prompt. Statements are
  written to be said at the front of a room ("Three of you felt the deadline was the real problem"),
  not filed in a dashboard ("Sentiment analysis indicates negative valence toward timelines"). The
  model is told never to name or number a participant, never to quote verbatim, and to count
  `supportCount` honestly _because that number decides whether a finding is safe to say aloud at
  all_. The gate is the backstop for when it does anyway.

- **Clamping support to the room size is an HONESTY guard, not a second gate.** It stops a model
  overstating "six of you" — but it can never suppress a finding, because the floor check
  guarantees some slot reached `minSupport`, `respondedCount <= participantCount`, and so a clamped
  count is always at or above the threshold. Worth stating because the opposite is easy to assume;
  a test pins it. The gate is the only thing that suppresses.

- **The synthesiser never throws.** A facilitator standing in front of a room does not need an
  exception — every failure returns an empty result and logs, the console shows "no synthesis yet",
  and they retry or carry on. The same contract as the routing selector, for the same reason.

- **It is given a long timeout and a large budget, unlike the routing selector.** Nobody is
  watching a spinner: the facilitator triggers it and keeps talking. Correctness beats latency here,
  which is the reverse of the fork the respondent waits at.

## What is built

**Schema** (migration `20260720110000`, hand-stripped, indexes verified): `AppExperienceMeeting`
(joinRef, status, currentStepId, breakoutStartedAt/EndsAt, facilitator), `AppExperienceInsight`
(kind, statement, detail, supportCount, ordinal, covered, visibleToRespondents),
`AppExperienceRun.meetingId`, breakout meta on the step (`durationSeconds` as the DEFAULT,
`briefing`, `synthesisFocus`), and the live clock on the meeting (`breakoutDurationSeconds`,
`breakoutGraceSeconds`).

**Settings**: `respondentInsightDisplay`, `consoleDisplayMode`, `breakoutGraceSeconds` — keys plus
defaults on the existing blob, no migration.

**Lib** (`experiences/meeting/`): `types.ts` (statuses, insight kinds, the three-phase clock,
`MeetingLiveState`), `anonymity.ts` (the k-anonymity gate), `lifecycle.ts` (transition rules +
`participantWindow`), `synthesis-material.ts` (what the synthesiser reads), `synthesise.ts` (the
agent call, the prompt, and the gated result).

**Tests**: 134 — the k-anonymity matrix including "visibility cannot override the gate"; every
lifecycle transition including the clock-is-advisory guard; the three-phase clock with both
boundaries and the fail-open case; the settings narrowing; the synthesis material including the
no-identity and object-value guards; and the synthesiser's gate, clamp, contiguous renumbering,
never-throws contract and prompt invariants; and the refinement-history narrowing.

**Service** (`_lib/meeting-service.ts`): meeting CRUD, the five transitions, `synthesiseAndStore`,
`joinMeeting`, `participantState`, and the audience-filtered reads.

**Routes**: `GET|POST /:id/meetings`, `POST /meetings/:id/actions`, `GET /meetings/:id/live` (the
poll, serving both audiences from one shape), `PATCH /meetings/:id/insights/:insightId`,
`POST /meetings/:id/join`, `GET /meetings/:id/participant`.

**UI**: the Meetings tab, the facilitator console (`presentation` mode for a shared screen), the
participant surface at `/m/<joinRef>`, and the respondent insight panel (tab or modal).

**Seed**: `082-meeting-synthesiser-agent`, applied.

## Design notes from the wiring

**The console polls; the clock ticks locally.** Two intervals, deliberately: a 3s poll for the
room's numbers, and a 1s tick that only re-renders the countdown. Asking the server every second
for a number the client can compute would be a request per participant per second across a room.

**A dropped poll keeps the last known state on screen.** A facilitator mid-sentence must not have
the room's numbers replaced by an error banner.

**Joining and answering are separate.** A participant gets a RUN the moment they arrive — so
"12 people are here" is true during the facilitator's introduction — and a SESSION only once a
breakout is running. Someone who joins early is present with nothing to answer, which is correct.

**A session is minted only when `canAnswer` is true.** Creating one during the grace window would
hand somebody a blank questionnaire they have seconds to fill, which is worse than nothing.

**Ending a breakout never extends a clock.** If the deadline has already passed, `endBreakout`
keeps it rather than moving it to now — otherwise pulling the room back would hand them grace they
had already used.

**Synthesis is fire-and-forget from every write path.** The facilitator pressing "pull them back"
is standing in front of a room; the room's attention is the scarce resource, not the server's.

**Regenerating REPLACES insights rather than appending.** A synthesis is a snapshot of what the
room said; running it again after more people finish should give the current picture, not accrete
stale findings beside fresh ones. The facilitator's `covered` marks are lost, which is the honest
trade — they referred to findings that no longer exist.

**Publishing to respondents defaults to off and is per-insight.** It is an editorial act taken in
the room with the finding in front of them, never a side effect of generation — and it cannot
defeat the support gate, which is checked independently.

## Deferred to F15.5b

Rooms within a breakout, each with a dedicated questionnaire, in individual **or single-scribe**
mode. Scribe mode changes who owns a session — one session representing a whole room rather than a
person — which is a real persistence change and should not ride along with the core loop.
